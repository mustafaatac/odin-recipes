Lentil Soup

Description

Lentil soup is one of the essential flavors of Turkish cuisine. Made with red lentils, onions, and carrots, this soup is enriched with the unique aroma of cumin and red pepper. A splash of fresh lemon juice adds a light and refreshing touch to the soup. Simple yet hearty, lentil soup offers a warm dose of happiness in every spoonful.

With its easy preparation and nourishing qualities, lentil soup can be served with crispy bread or sprinkled with chili flakes. Loved for both its taste and its comforting properties, this classic soup makes a warm start to any meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup red lentils (washed and drained)
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 1 medium carrot (peeled and chopped)
  • 1 medium potato (peeled and chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or butter
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon paprika
  • ½ teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 5 cups water or vegetable broth (adjust for desired consistency)
  • Juice of half a lemon (for serving)
  • Optional: Red pepper flakes or dried mint (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Wash the red lentils thoroughly and drain them. Chop the onion, carrot, and potato into small pieces.
  2. Sauté the Base: Heat olive oil or butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until softened and translucent. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for another 1-2 minutes to enhance its flavor.
  3. Add the Vegetables and Lentils: Add the chopped carrot, potato, and red lentils to the pot. Stir to combine everything well.
  4. Add Liquid and Seasonings: Pour in the water or vegetable broth and stir. Add cumin, paprika, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Cook the Soup: Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer for about 25-30 minutes, or until the lentils and vegetables are soft and tender.
  6. Blend the Soup: Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth and creamy. Alternatively, you can transfer it to a regular blender in batches, then return it to the pot.
  7. Adjust Consistency: If the soup is too thick, add a bit more water or broth until it reaches your desired consistency. Simmer for another 2-3 minutes.
  8. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls. Drizzle with fresh lemon juice and garnish with red pepper flakes or dried mint if desired. Serve warm with crusty bread on the side.
