Lahmacun

Description

Discover the magic of Lahmacun, a true Turkish classic that’s as fun to eat as it is delicious. This thin, crispy flatbread is topped with a mouthwatering mix of seasoned minced meat, fresh tomatoes, onions, and fragrant spices. Served hot out of the oven, it’s best enjoyed rolled up with a splash of lemon and a handful of fresh herbs or veggies – the perfect balance of bright, bold flavors.

Lahmacun isn’t just food; it’s a slice of Turkish culture. Whether you’re grabbing a quick bite or sharing it with friends and family, this dish is all about simple ingredients coming together in the most flavorful way. One bite, and you’ll see why it’s been a favorite for generations!

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups of flour
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of dry yeast
  • 3/4 cup of warm water
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil

For the Topping:

  • 250 grams of minced meat (preferably fatty lamb or beef)
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped or grated)
  • 1 tomato (grated)
  • 1 green and red pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of pepper paste
  • 1 tablespoon of tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup of parsley (finely chopped)
  • 1 garlic clove (optional, finely chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cumin
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 2-3 tablespoons of olive oil or water (to make the mixture smoother)

Steps

  1. Add the red peppers, green peppers, onions, and tomatoes to a food processor.
  2. Blend them until they are chopped finely, but not completely pureed.
  3. Mix in the tomato paste, pepper paste, olive oil, and water. Finally, add the minced meat and mix everything thoroughly. For a flavorful Lahmacun, the mixture should have a slightly runny consistency. If it’s too thick, you can add a little more water.
  4. If you have time, let the mixture rest in the refrigerator for a while.
  5. While the mixture is resting, start preparing the dough. Pour the water into a bowl, add the salt and sugar, and mix well. Gradually add flour and knead until the dough reaches a medium-firm consistency.
  6. Shape the dough into a cylinder and divide it into equal portions. To ensure all Lahmacuns are the same size, make sure each dough ball weighs the same. Let the dough balls rest for a while.
  7. Roll out the dough balls to the size of a dinner plate. It’s important to roll them as thin as possible. Meanwhile, preheat your oven to 250°C (482°F) with the fan on and the top and bottom heat settings active. Let the oven heat up properly.
  8. Spread the minced meat mixture evenly over the rolled-out dough. Place them on the bottom rack of the oven along with parchment paper and bake for 7-10 minutes until the bottoms are golden brown.
