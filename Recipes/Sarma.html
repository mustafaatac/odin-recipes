Sarma

Description

Sarma, a beloved Turkish classic, features grape leaves wrapped around a flavorful filling of rice, herbs, and spices. With hints of dill, parsley, and lemon, it’s a perfect balance of zesty and savory flavors, enjoyed warm with yogurt or cold as a mezze.

What makes Sarma special is the care in its preparation, often made together with family. This timeless dish is more than food – it’s a symbol of Turkish hospitality and tradition, offering a taste of culture in every bite.

Ingredients

For the Filling

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 medium onions, finely chopped
  • ⅔ cup short-grain rice, rinsed
  • ½ bunch fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • ½ bunch fresh dill, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried mint
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For Assembly

  • 40 fresh or jarred grape leaves, rinsed and stems removed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice

Steps

  1. First, brined grape leaves are soaked in hot water for 2-3 minutes, rinsed, and drained in a colander.
  2. Next, onions are grated into a large bowl.
  3. Olive oil is poured over the onions.
  4. Washed rice is added to the bowl.
  5. Spices, tomato paste, and salt are incorporated, followed by finely chopped parsley, and the mixture is stirred well (the filling will be raw).
  6. A small amount of the filling is placed on the wide part of the grape leaf, which is then rolled like a cylinder, folding the sides inward to secure the filling.
  7. This process is repeated until all grape leaves are used.
  8. The rolled grape leaves are placed in a pot. Olive oil and lemon slices are added on top.
  9. Hot water is poured into the pot until it slightly covers the rolls.
  10. To prevent the rolls from unrolling, a plate smaller than the pot is placed on top of the grape leaves.
  11. The Sarma is cooked over low heat until the filling is tender.
